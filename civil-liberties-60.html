Civil Liberties in 60 Seconds

Constitutional rights, surveillance, and privacy — explained in one minute, in plain language, by a clinical law professor who litigates these issues.

Visit the Website → Watch on YouTube →

Big legal questions shouldn't require a law degree to understand.

Civil Liberties in 60 Seconds takes the surveillance and civil-liberties issues Catherine Crump works on — the Fourth Amendment, free speech, policing technology, and privacy — and turns them into short, shareable videos anyone can follow.

Each episode breaks down a right, a news story, or a "know your rights" question in about a minute, cutting through legal jargon without dumbing down the substance.

New episodes are posted regularly on YouTube. Follow along and share the ones that matter to you.

What you'll find

  • Know-your-rights explainers
  • Surveillance & privacy news, decoded
  • Fourth & First Amendment basics
  • Plain-language takes on new court rulings

Educational content — not legal advice.
