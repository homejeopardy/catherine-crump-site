About

Catherine Crump

I'm a clinical professor at UC Berkeley School of Law, where I direct the Samuelson Law, Technology & Public Policy Clinic and co-direct the Berkeley Center for Law & Technology. My work lives where technology, surveillance, and constitutional rights collide — police use of location data and license plate readers, government data dragnets, and free speech online.

Most of what I do is about making these questions legible and consequential outside the academy. I litigate and file public-records suits, write reports and white papers, advise lawmakers, and talk to the press about what new surveillance tools mean for ordinary people. I've served as an adviser to the White House Domestic Policy Council, and before Berkeley I was a staff attorney at the ACLU. I also make Civil Liberties in 60 Seconds, short explainers that apply longstanding constitutional rights to current events.
